Peak Fettle — Avatar Art-Direction Concepts

TICKET-096, Phase 1. Four distinct directions for the customizable cartoon avatar, three sample characters each. The brief: bespoke, fun, simple, funny. Pick one direction (or mix two) and the layered, parametric avatar system gets built against it — no avatar code ships until you choose.
⛔ Decision needed from you: choose an art direction below. Everything here is a sketch to react to, not final art — once you pick, a proper artist/asset pipeline produces the full option set (many hairstyles, hats, etc.) in the chosen style.

A · Flat Chunky

bold • geometric • modern
Big round heads, flat color blocks, thick rounded outlines. Reads clearly at tiny sizes (rankings rows, group lists). Friendly and confident — the "default modern app avatar" but with personality.
warm skin · brown crop
deep skin · round glasses
light skin · beanie
Why it works: instantly legible at 28px, cheap to produce a large option library, and on-trend. Risk: can feel generic if the feature set isn't pushed (lean into expressive eyes + props).

B · Soft Blob

minimal • silly • squishy
Almost no anatomy — a rounded blob, dot eyes, a single expressive mouth line. Maximum "funny/simple." Personality comes from color + one or two props. Charming and very cheap to extend.
mango blob
teal blob · antennae
grape blob · specs
Why it works: the funniest + simplest, scales to hundreds of combos trivially, very "ownable." Risk: less "this is me" realism — fewer human features to customize (skin tone matters less). Great for a playful brand, weaker if users want a literal self-portrait.

C · Bold Sticker

heavy outline • vivid • retro
Thick black ink lines, saturated fills, a die-cut "sticker" feel. High energy, slightly retro/streetwear. Pops on a dark theme (Deep Ocean) and feels collectible — pairs naturally with unlockable cosmetics.
classic ink
cap · shades · 'stache
beard · big curls
Why it works: the most distinctive + "premium/collectible" — strong fit with the cosmetics shop (cosmetic_items). Looks great on the dark UI. Risk: heavier to draw each asset cleanly; busy at very small sizes.

D · Peak Pals (on-brand mascot)

branded • gym buddies • warm
Rounded "buddy" characters built around Peak Fettle cues — headbands, a little mountain/peak motif, dumbbell accents. Less a literal self-portrait, more a companion that grows with you. Ties the avatar into the brand and the "training companion" idea.
headband · peaks
spotting a dumbbell
PR sparkle
Why it works: the only direction that builds brand equity — the avatar becomes a recognizable Peak Fettle "pal." Strong for retention/streak moments. Risk: furthest from "a face that looks like me"; needs the most original character design up front.

The customizer (same in every direction)

Whichever style you pick, the build is a layered, parametric system: each category is a swappable layer with many options, plus a Randomize button and live preview. We serialize the option set (not a rendered image) so it ships in the local-first backup (TICKET-094) and re-renders identically on any device.
