## 1. Sport overview

Powerlifting is contested in three lifts — the **back squat, bench press, and deadlift** — and scored on the **1-rep-max total** (best single in each, summed), adjusted for bodyweight via coefficient formulas (DOTS, IPF GL points).

**What it optimizes:** maximal force production — low-velocity, high-load — in three specific barbell movement patterns.

**Primary adaptations targeted:** maximal strength (both neural — rate coding, motor-unit recruitment, intermuscular coordination — and structural — myofibrillar hypertrophy of prime movers); motor skill in the three competition lifts (groove, bracing, bar path); and connective-tissue/joint resilience to tolerate near-maximal loading.

**Key performance determinants:** cross-sectional area of prime movers (quads, glutes, adductors, spinal erectors, pecs, delts, triceps, lats, hamstrings); neural efficiency and technique under maximal load; limb proportions and leverages (largely fixed, but they inform technique); bracing / intra-abdominal pressure and whole-body tension; fatigue management to express strength on one day; and bodyweight management relative to weight class.

**RPE anchors used throughout this doc:** RPE 10 = true max, no reps left. RPE 9 = 1 rep left. RPE 8 = 2 reps left. An RPE-8 triple is roughly 85–87% 1RM. Reserve RPE 9.5–10 for peaks and meets.

## 2. Programming by experience tier

> Tier is set by recovery capacity, not time. A lifter is a **beginner** while they still add load nearly every session; **intermediate** once progress is measured week-to-week or month-to-month; **advanced** once they are near their genetic strength ceiling with only small annual gains.

### BEGINNER (roughly first 6–12 months)

| Variable | Prescription |
|---|---|
| Frequency | 3 full-body days/week, non-consecutive (e.g., Mon/Wed/Fri). Squat ~3×/wk, bench ~3×/wk, deadlift 1–2×/wk (it recovers slower). |
| Volume | ~3 working sets per lift per session. ≈9 squat sets/wk, ≈9 bench sets/wk, ≈3–5 deadlift sets/wk. |
| Intensity | ~70–85% 1RM, RPE 7–8. No true maximal or minimal loads needed. |
| Rep scheme | Mostly sets of 5 (classic 3×5; deadlift 1×5). |
| Periodization | **Linear progression (LP)** — all qualities improve at once; no weekly waving needed. |
| Progression | Add load **every session**. Typical jumps: squat ~10 lb early then 5 lb; deadlift 15–20 lb early then 10 then 5; bench/press 5 lb, dropping to 2.5 lb microplates when 5 lb stalls. |
| Deload | Reactive, not scheduled. On a miss, repeat the weight or drop ~10% and re-climb. Three straight stalls on a lift despite a deload and adequate food/sleep = LP is over; graduate to intermediate. |
| Weekly time | ~3–4.5 hours (3 sessions, 60–90 min). |

### INTERMEDIATE (roughly 1–3 years)

| Variable | Prescription |
|---|---|
| Frequency | Squat and bench each 2–3×/wk; deadlift 1–2×/wk (often one heavy + one volume/variation day). 4 days/week is the common structure. |
| Volume | ~10–20 working sets per lift per week, periodized — start a block near MEV (~10–12 hard sets), ramp toward MRV, then deload. Accessory work for quads/chest/back ~12–16 sets/wk. |
| Intensity | Waved. Volume work 70–82% (RPE 6–8); intensity work 83–92% (RPE 8–9). |
| Rep scheme | Triples, fives, and singles–doubles on heavy days; 6–8s for accessories. First main-lift variations enter here (pause squat, close-grip bench, deficit/block pulls). |
| Periodization | **Weekly or Daily Undulating Periodization (WUP/DUP)** — e.g., a volume day + an intensity day per lift each week. Texas Method (volume / light / intensity day) is the archetypal intermediate template. |
| Progression | Weekly or per-mesocycle — add load when an RPE target is hit at the prescribed reps, or run a 3–4 week wave then raise training maxes ~2.5–5%. |
| Deload | Every 4–6 weeks, or when recovery markers drop. Standard deload: cut sets ~⅓–⅔, drop RPE ~1 point, drop load ~5%. |
| Weekly time | ~5–7 hours (4 sessions, 75–105 min). |

### ADVANCED (3+ years, near genetic ceiling)

| Variable | Prescription |
|---|---|
| Frequency | Squat/bench often 3–4×/wk (incl. variations); deadlift 1–2×/wk plus pulling variations. Most train 4–6 days/week. |
| Volume | High and tightly periodized between MEV and MRV — main lifts can reach 15–25+ hard sets/week at peak accumulation, dropped sharply in intensification/peak. Volume is the primary lever to manipulate. |
| Intensity | Full spectrum, heavily autoregulated. Accumulation 70–80%; transmutation 80–90%; realization/peak 90–100%+. |
| Rep scheme | Heavy singles, doubles, triples on the competition lifts; fives-and-up confined to variations and accessories. |
| Periodization | **Block periodization** — Accumulation (3–5 wk, high volume) → Transmutation/Intensification (higher intensity, lower volume, more specificity) → Realization/Peak (very low volume, very high intensity, taper). Often combined with DUP inside blocks. |
| Progression | Across blocks and meet cycles. Rotate variations to address sticking points; raise training maxes only when warranted. |
| Deload | Back-off week every 3–5 weeks, plus a competition taper (1–3 weeks: cut volume sharply, keep some intensity). Larger/older/stronger athletes need longer tapers. |
| Weekly time | ~6–10+ hours (4–6 sessions, 90–150 min). |

## 3. Ideal exercises + equipment substitution ladders

Each ladder runs: **full commercial gym → home gym (barbell + rack) → minimal (dumbbells / bands) → bodyweight / no equipment.** Substitutions train the same pattern; they do not perfectly replace the competition lift.

### Competition lifts (non-negotiable, highest value)

**Back Squat** — the contested lift; primary driver of total lower-body strength.
Barbell back squat in a rack with safeties → identical (barbell + squat stands) → heavy goblet squat, double-DB squat, or DB Bulgarian split squat (add bands to a goblet squat for resistance) → pistol squats, Bulgarian split squats, tempo/paused bodyweight squats, shrimp squats.

**Bench Press** — the contested upper-body lift.
Barbell bench with rack + safeties/spotter → identical (barbell + bench + stands) → heavy DB bench press, DB floor press, banded push-ups → push-ups → feet-elevated → deficit/ring → one-arm progressions (weighted via backpack).

**Deadlift** — the contested hip-hinge; the largest single load lifted.
Barbell conventional or sumo deadlift → identical (barbell + plates; a rack is not required) → heavy DB Romanian deadlift, DB sumo deadlift, single-leg DB RDL, banded hinge → single-leg RDL, hip thrust/glute bridge variations, back extensions, Nordic hamstring curls (eccentric).

### High-value accessories

**Front Squat** — quads and upright posture; carries to the squat and brace.
Barbell front squat → barbell front squat → double-DB front-rack squat or goblet squat → heel-elevated tempo squat, Bulgarian split squat.

**Romanian Deadlift** — posterior chain and deadlift eccentric strength.
Barbell RDL → barbell RDL → DB or single-leg DB RDL, banded RDL → single-leg RDL, Nordic curls, back extensions.

**Overhead / Strict Press** — shoulder and triceps strength; supports bench lockout and upper back.
Barbell overhead press → barbell overhead press → standing DB shoulder press, band overhead press → pike push-ups progressing toward handstand push-ups.

**Incline / Close-Grip Bench Press** — upper chest, triceps, bench mid-range and lockout.
Barbell incline or close-grip bench → same → incline DB press, close-grip DB floor press → decline (feet-down) push-ups, diamond push-ups.

**Barbell Row / Pull-Up (upper-back work)** — yoke and lat strength; supports squat bracing, bench stability, deadlift lockout.
Barbell row, pull-ups, lat pulldown, chest-supported row → barbell row, pull-up bar → single-arm DB row, band pulldown, inverted row → inverted rows, pull-ups/chin-ups, towel rows.

**Good Morning** — spinal erectors, hamstrings, glutes; reinforces the hinge and braced back.
Barbell good morning → barbell good morning → banded good morning, DB RDL substitute → back extensions, bird-dogs, Superman holds.

**Split Squat / Lunge** — unilateral quad/glute strength; addresses left–right asymmetry and knee/hip resilience.
Barbell or DB split squat → barbell or DB split squat → DB walking lunge, Bulgarian split squat → bodyweight Bulgarian split squat, step-ups, reverse lunges.

**Triceps + Biceps work** — elbow health and bench lockout (triceps); biceps protect against deadlift/row tendon strain.
Cable pushdowns, skull crushers, curls → EZ-bar/DB extensions and curls → band pushdowns, band curls, DB versions → close-grip/diamond push-ups, bench dips, isometric towel curls.

**Core / anti-extension** (planks, hanging leg raises, ab wheel) — bracing capacity transferring to all three lifts.
Ab wheel, hanging leg raise, cable crunch → ab wheel, hanging leg raise → band Pallof press, weighted plank → planks, hollow holds, leg raises, dead bugs.

## 4. Common mistakes by tier

**Beginner:** program-hopping or using advanced templates instead of riding simple LP to exhaustion; adding load faster than technique allows; too much accessory volume diluting recovery; under-eating and under-sleeping; treating one missed rep as the end of LP instead of deloading and re-climbing.

**Intermediate:** staying on LP long after it stopped working; training at RPE 9–10 every session; skipping deloads; program-hopping with no logbook; neglecting weak-point variations; unbounded volume creep.

**Advanced:** chasing intensity (constant heavy singles) at the expense of a volume/hypertrophy base; poor fatigue management and mistimed tapers; insufficient hypertrophy work to keep prime movers growing; year-round high specificity causing overuse injury; ignoring autoregulation through bad recovery weeks.
