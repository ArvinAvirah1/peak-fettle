## Settled points

- ~80/20 intensity distribution holds across tiers. Pyramidal in base/build, more polarized near racing for many runners.
- 10% weekly volume increase is a soft cap, not a target. Hold steady weeks are valuable.
- Strength training (2–3×/wk, heavy, ≥90% 1RM) + plyometrics improves running economy ~2–6% and does not add meaningful aerobic fatigue when programmed away from key runs.
- Most amateur underperformance traces to the "moderate-intensity rut" — easy too hard, hard too easy.
- Advanced runners are *more* prone to overtraining than intermediates due to motivation/discipline.
