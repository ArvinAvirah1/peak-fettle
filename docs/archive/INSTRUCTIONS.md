Peak Fettle is a fitness tracking and coaching app built around the belief that consistency is the most critical factor in fitness progress. It serves users across all fitness disciplines — weightlifting, running, cycling, swimming, team sports, and more — with tools that are both deeply personal and competitively motivating.

## Progress Tracking

Tracking is tailored to the user's activity. For weightlifting, the app logs sets, reps, and weight over time and calculates both a raw 1RM estimate and a normalized strength score. Users can choose between industry-standard scoring (Wilks/DOTS, familiar to experienced lifters) and a proprietary Peak Fettle score that factors in volume, progressive overload, and consistency — making it accessible to beginners as well. For cardio disciplines, the app tracks split times, pace trends, and consistency. Wearable device integration (Apple Watch, Garmin, etc.) is on the roadmap as a future feature; at launch, stats are entered manually or via the app's session logger.

## Competitive Percentiles

Users can opt into a competitive mode that shows where they rank relative to people in similar situations. Percentiles always account for age, gender (with an opt-out for a unisex scale), and total years in the sport — recognizing that relative gains naturally diminish as someone advances. All users are ranked within a cohort matched to their experience level and demographic, so a beginner is never compared against a 10-year veteran. This keeps early feedback encouraging while remaining honest about long-term progress.

## Personalized Plans (Paid)

Paid users complete an opening survey covering: fitness goals across as many disciplines as they choose, current fitness level and injury history, available equipment and facilities, preferred session length and weekly availability, and preferred exercise types. The AI generates a fully personalized program based on these inputs and recommendations from seasoned coaches and trainers. Plans are modifiable by the user at any time — exercises can be swapped, volume adjusted, and days rescheduled — because enjoyable plans are consistent plans. The AI adapts the program over time based on logged performance.

Body composition goals are handled with care. Users can set a weight goal (cut, bulk, or recomp) at any point, and the AI will assess feasibility and suggest an attainable target given the user's current situation — explaining why aggressive short-term goals often lead to relapse. The user always has final say, but the AI guides them toward sustainable outcomes. Goals can be updated at any time as the user's priorities evolve.

## Habit & Streak Tracking

The app tracks workout consistency with a daily streak system rooted in behavioral science. Users are encouraged to treat physical presence as the baseline — even a five-minute gym visit counts, because building the routine itself rewires the habit loop. The app explains this philosophy to users directly. Missed sessions are handled without shame: users have a make-up window within the same week to preserve their streak. For genuine emergencies (exams, illness, travel), a manual override is available to protect the streak without requiring a make-up. Streaks are lost only if two sessions are missed in a week with no make-up and no override flagged.

## Free Tier

The free tier includes full progress tracking and competitive percentiles — already ahead of most competitors. It also includes a library of static training templates (Push/Pull/Legs, Upper/Lower splits) that users can follow as a structured starting point. These are fixed plans, not AI-generated, but they are well-designed and immediately usable. The paid tier unlocks fully personalized, adaptive programming.
