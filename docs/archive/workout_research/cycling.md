## 1. Sport overview

Cycling performance is power output relative to resistance, governed by:

- **VO2max / maximal aerobic power (MAP)** — the aerobic ceiling; sets the upper limit of sustainable power. Trained by Zone 5 intervals.
- **Functional Threshold Power (FTP) / lactate threshold** — the highest power sustainable for ~45–60 min; the central reference metric in cycling. Zone 4 is the "money zone" for road performance. Threshold work improves lactate clearance and raises FTP.
- **Efficiency / economy** — the metabolic cost of producing power. Improved by volume and strength work.
- **Power-to-weight ratio (W/kg)** — decisive for climbing and road racing.

Secondary determinants: anaerobic capacity (Z6), neuromuscular sprint power (Z7), durability (power retention deep into long rides), and mitochondrial density from Zone 2 volume.

**Key structural difference from running:** cycling is **non-weight-bearing and low-impact**, so riders tolerate much higher training volumes (in hours) without the same injury risk. This shapes its programming — volume can climb higher and faster than in running.

**The 80/20 foundation** applies here too — ~80% low intensity, ~20% moderate-to-hard. The dominant amateur error is the "sweet-spot trap" — living in the moderate Z3 "no man's land" so easy rides aren't easy.

## 2. Zone model (Coggan 7-zone power model — % of FTP)

- **Z1 Active Recovery** — <55% FTP. Easy spinning.
- **Z2 Endurance** — 56–75% FTP. The aerobic base zone; drives mitochondrial adaptation; the bulk of polarized training.
- **Z3 Tempo** — 76–90% FTP. "Sweet spot" sits at the top of Z3 into low Z4 (~88–94% FTP).
- **Z4 Threshold** — 91–105% FTP. Raises FTP; the "money zone."
- **Z5 VO2max** — 106–120% FTP. 3–8 min intervals; lifts the aerobic ceiling.
- **Z6 Anaerobic** — 121–150% FTP. 30 sec–2 min efforts.
- **Z7 Neuromuscular** — >150% FTP. Sprints, max <15 sec.

A simplified 3-zone polarized model (below LT1 / between / above LT2) is also widely used.

## 3. Programming by experience tier

### BEGINNER (new to structured cycling)

| Variable | Prescription |
|---|---|
| Frequency | 3 rides/week. |
| Volume | ~3–6 hours/week. A reasonable entry point: comfortable riding ~2 hours and riding 2–3×/week. |
| Intensity | Mostly Zone 2 endurance; introduce light intervals only once consistent. The priority is simply accumulating aerobic time. |
| Key sessions | 1–2 Zone 2 endurance rides 45–90 min; 1 longer weekend ride ~1.5–2 hr with a few short efforts to break it up. Indoor sessions ≤60 min. |
| Periodization | Simple linear base build; formal periodization not needed for general fitness. |
| Progression | Increase weekly volume gradually; build duration before intensity. (Cycling tolerates more than the running 10%/week guideline, but it remains a sensible soft cap.) |
| Deload | Every 3–4 weeks ease volume. |
| Weekly time | ~3–6 hrs/week. |

### INTERMEDIATE (1–3 yrs, structured training, some events)

| Variable | Prescription |
|---|---|
| Frequency | 4–5 rides/week. |
| Volume | ~6–10 hours/week. |
| Intensity | ~80/20. Many intermediate plans use a **pyramidal** distribution in base/build (more Z3/sweet-spot volume) — time-efficient and effective for raising FTP — then shift toward **polarized** as events approach. Solid gains are possible in <5 hrs/week using 2 × 20 min sweet-spot intervals. |
| Key sessions/wk | 1 long endurance ride (2–3+ hr, Zone 2); 1 sweet-spot or threshold session (2 × 20 min at 88–95% FTP, or 3 × 12 min at Z4); 1 VO2max session every 1–2 weeks (5 × 4 min at 106–120% FTP, 4 min recovery); remaining rides Z1–Z2. |
| Periodization | Friel framework: Prep → Base (aerobic volume, muscular endurance) → Build (threshold + VO2max, event-specific) → Peak (sharpen, reduce volume) → Race/Taper. Mesocycles ~3–4 weeks. Common practice: pyramidal in base/build → polarized toward racing. |
| Fatigue management | Separate energy systems — avoid sweet-spot during pure base (it adds fatigue without extra aerobic benefit vs straight Z2); reserve it for build. |
| Deload | **3:1 loading** (3 build weeks, 1 recovery week) is the common default; recovery week cuts volume ~40–60%, keeps a little intensity. Personalize to fatigue (every 4–8 weeks depending on load). |
| Weekly time | ~6–10 hrs/week (or <5 hrs/week with a time-efficient indoor plan). |

### ADVANCED (3+ yrs, racing/competitive)

| Variable | Prescription |
|---|---|
| Frequency | 5–7 rides/week, sometimes doubles. |
| Volume | ~10–15+ hours/week. |
| Intensity | ~80/20, periodized — pyramidal-heavy in base/build to maximize FTP and aerobic load, transitioning to polarized in the final ~8 weeks before key events. (Polarized shows an edge for VO2peak mainly in blocks <12 weeks; over longer terms pyramidal and polarized perform similarly for TT and threshold power.) |
| Key sessions | 2–3 quality sessions/week — threshold/over-unders, VO2max intervals (5–6 × 5 min at Z5; 30/15s), anaerobic/race-specific efforts, plus long Z2 rides for durability and a sprint/neuromuscular session as needed. |
| Periodization | Full multi-mesocycle season, often double-periodized for two race peaks; "8 weeks pyramidal + 8 weeks polarized" is a documented effective block structure. |
| Progression | Monitor with power (CTL/ATL/TSB), HRV, resting HR, mood; year-round training mandates regular rest weeks. |
| Deload | Every 3–4 weeks (or personalized 4–8); volume −40–60%. |
| Weekly time | ~10–15+ hrs/week. |

## 4. Key sessions, equipment alternatives, and strength support

### Highest-value workout types
1. **Zone 2 endurance ride** — 60 min–4+ hr at 56–75% FTP. Drives mitochondrial density, capillarization, fat oxidation, durability. The foundation; the bulk of all volume.
2. **Long ride** — extended Z2 (intermediate 2–3 hr; advanced 3–5+ hr). Builds durability and fatigue resistance.
3. **Sweet-spot intervals** — 2 × 20 min (or 3 × 12 min) at ~88–95% FTP. Time-efficient FTP builder; best in build, not deep base.
4. **Threshold intervals** — 3 × 12–15 min or 2 × 20 min at 91–105% FTP; over-unders. Directly raises FTP — the "money zone."
5. **VO2max intervals** — 4–6 × 3–5 min at 106–120% FTP with near-equal recovery; or 30/15s. Lifts the aerobic ceiling.
6. **Anaerobic / neuromuscular** — 30 sec–2 min at Z6, or short maximal sprints (Z7). Race-specific punch.

### Equipment ladder
**Outdoor road bike** — gold standard for skill, terrain, durability. **Indoor trainers:** *smart trainer* (bike mounts on it, controls resistance, ERG mode holds exact power — best for structured intervals via Zwift/TrainerRoad/Rouvy); *dumb/wheel-on trainer* (resistance via roller, no auto-control); *rollers* (improve balance and pedaling smoothness, less resistance control); *stationary / spin bike* (fully self-contained; heavy flywheel + adjustable resistance; usable for all interval structures, though power accuracy varies — pair with a power meter or use HR/perceived effort). All indoor options are excellent substitutes; indoor sessions are typically kept ≤60 min and interval-structured. **No equipment at all:** maintain aerobic and leg-specific fitness with bodyweight HIIT/circuits — air squats, jump squats, lunges, step-ups, burpees, mountain climbers, high knees — done as intervals (e.g., 30s work / 30s rest) to mimic both Z2 and interval stimulus; jump rope; stair climbing and brisk incline walking/hiking transfer reasonably to cycling's aerobic demands.

### Strength-training support (evidence-based — improves MAP, threshold power, sprint power, efficiency)
Heavy strength training improves cyclists' maximal aerobic power, power at threshold, short-duration/sprint power, and efficiency — without harming endurance. A 2024 RCT found heavy off-bike squats and high-intensity on-bike resistance work produced *similar* gains, so a barbell is not strictly required. Protocol: **2–3 sessions/week**, heavy low-rep lower-body focus, **3–5 sets of 4–8 reps at ≥80% 1RM**; concentrate it in the off-season, maintain (reduced volume) in race season.

Equipment ladder for the strength work: **Full gym** — back squat, leg press, deadlift/RDL, split squat (heavy 3–5 × 4–8) plus core. **Dumbbells/kettlebell** — goblet/double-KB squat, single-leg RDL, heavy walking lunges, step-ups, hip thrusts. **Bodyweight** — pistol squats / Bulgarian split squats, single-leg hip thrusts/glute bridges, step-ups, calf raises; plus the equipment-free on-bike alternative — **big-gear, low-cadence (~50–60 rpm) seated intervals on the trainer**, which research shows builds pedaling-specific strength comparably to off-bike squats. Core throughout: planks, side planks, dead bugs, bird dogs (cyclists need core stability for power transfer and back health).

## 5. Common mistakes by tier

**Beginner:** adding intensity before building any aerobic base; no Zone 2 patience; irregular riding; ignoring bike fit (causes knee/back/neck issues); no strength or core work; treating every ride as moderately hard.

**Intermediate:** the **sweet-spot trap** — living in Z3 year-round so easy rides aren't easy and the "no man's land" between LT1 and LT2 dominates; doing sweet-spot during pure base when straight Z2 gives the same aerobic benefit with less fatigue; skipping recovery weeks; under-fueling long rides.

**Advanced:** chronic accumulated fatigue from year-round training without disciplined rest weeks → stagnation or overtraining; insufficient taper/peak; over-relying on indoor intensity and neglecting long Z2 durability work; racing too frequently without down-weeks; chasing FTP numbers while neglecting durability and recovery monitoring.
