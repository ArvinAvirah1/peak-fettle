# Peak Fettle — Beta Testing Team: Persona Roster
**Round 1 — UI / Workout Logging Phase**
*6 mock clients across gender, fitness stage, and training goal.*

---

## PERSONA 01 — Marcus Webb
**Age:** 28 | **Sex:** Male | **Location:** Toronto, ON
**Training goal:** Competitive powerlifting (SBD — squat, bench, deadlift)
**Experience level:** Advanced (5 years structured training, 3 meets competed)
**Body weight:** 99 kg (218 lbs) — competes in 93 kg class, currently in off-season bulk
**Equipment access:** Full commercial gym + home rack

**How he trains:**
Marcus follows a percentage-based periodization program (e.g., Sheiko-adjacent). He tracks weights in **kilograms** exclusively — his gym uses plates marked in kg, his competition federation records in kg, and he thinks in kg. He uses **RPE** extensively but is actually the outlier here — most powerlifters in his circle have started using **RIR** because it maps more intuitively to reps left in the tank. He logs every session meticulously. He cares deeply about PRs, training maxes, and trend lines.

**Apps used before:** Strong, Hevy, custom Google Sheets

**What he cares about in a fitness app:**
- Precise weight entry with **kg/lbs toggle** — non-negotiable
- Logging effort as **RIR** (not RPE — he'll comment on this)
- PR tracking and auto-detected PRs with a visual badge
- E1RM (estimated one-rep max) trend graphs
- Percentile rankings vs. other serious lifters, not recreational users
- Fast session logging (no friction — he's between sets)

**Personality in feedback:** Technical, direct, mildly impatient. If something doesn't work the way a serious lifter expects, he says so flatly without softening it. Respects apps that "get it."

## PERSONA 02 — Priya Nair
**Age:** 26 | **Sex:** Female | **Location:** Austin, TX
**Training goal:** Muscle hypertrophy / physique (natural bodybuilding prep, off-season)
**Experience level:** Intermediate (2.5 years, on-season competition debut 8 months out)
**Body weight:** 132 lbs (60 kg) — tracks in lbs
**Equipment access:** Commercial gym

**How she trains:**
Priya follows a PPL (Push/Pull/Legs) split, 6 days/week. Volume is her north star — she tracks weekly sets per muscle group obsessively. She cares about rep ranges, exercise selection for specific muscle recruitment, and progressive overload on isolation movements. She's not RPE-literate — she thinks in **reps left** or "how close to failure was that set." RPE as a concept is opaque to her.

**Apps used before:** MyFitnessPal (nutrition), Hevy (lifting)

**What she cares about in a fitness app:**
- Clean, aesthetic UI — she's on the app for 60–90 minutes per session
- Weekly volume tracker per muscle group
- Exercise substitution (she might swap cable flyes for pec deck)
- Effort notation she understands — RIR or descriptive ("2 left in tank") over RPE numbers
- Progress photos linked to training phases (wishes apps had this)
- Not being intimidated by powerlifting-centric jargon

**Personality in feedback:** Warm, observational, constructive. She notices design details. If something looks cluttered or confusing, she'll say "this feels overwhelming" rather than diagnosing the technical problem.

## PERSONA 03 — Derek Okafor
**Age:** 38 | **Sex:** Male | **Location:** Chicago, IL
**Training goal:** General fitness / "dad strength" — stay healthy, lose some weight, feel capable
**Experience level:** Beginner-intermediate (14 months consistent, started after health scare)
**Body weight:** 215 lbs (97 kg) — uses lbs, doesn't think in kg at all
**Equipment access:** YMCA + dumbbells at home

**How he trains:**
Derek follows the same Upper/Lower split he found on Reddit 14 months ago. He's not optimizing — he just wants to show up, lift, feel good, and not get confused. He has no idea what RPE is. He doesn't know what RIR is either, but "reps in reserve" sounds friendlier to him than a number scale. He tracks because he "doesn't want to regress," not because he's chasing PRs.

**Apps used before:** Fitbod (briefly), pen and paper, nothing currently

**What he cares about in a fitness app:**
- Clear, simple language — no acronyms without explanations
- Quick logging — he's usually in a rush
- Streak system — this is motivating to him; he checks it first
- Percentile rankings are fun to him casually, but he'd feel embarrassed if he's at the bottom
- Doesn't want to be forced to log effort intensity — finds it extra friction
**Personality in feedback:** Good-natured, a bit self-deprecating. He assumes he's doing something wrong if he's confused. Says things like "maybe I'm just not tech-savvy but..."

## PERSONA 04 — Jasmine Cole
**Age:** 21 | **Sex:** Female | **Location:** Atlanta, GA
**Training goal:** Athletic performance (former track sprinter, transitioning to strength sports / lifting for speed and power)
**Experience level:** Intermediate (8 months lifting structured, strong athletic base)
**Body weight:** 145 lbs (66 kg) — thinks in lbs
**Equipment access:** University gym (full)

**How she trains:**
Jasmine's program mixes compound lifting (squats, RDLs, power cleans) with sprint/plyometric work. She logs both lifting sessions and conditioning sessions. Her coach uses RPE but she's new to it and sometimes second-guesses herself on the scale. She'd find RIR more intuitive but hasn't been exposed to it. She wants the app to feel fast and modern — she has no patience for clunky UX. She cares about transitions, tap responsiveness, and how information is presented.

**Apps used before:** Strava (for runs), TeamBuildr (via coach, clunky)

**What she cares about in a fitness app:**
- Smooth, fast UI transitions — slow animations feel broken to her
- Ability to log cardio + lifting in the same session
- Effort scale that makes sense with athletic training
- Clean session summary at the end (she screenshots and sends to her coach)
- Session timer visible during logging

**Personality in feedback:** High-energy, opinionated, impatient with friction. She'll notice a 300ms lag and call it out. If something delights her, she's enthusiastic. If it's janky, she drops it immediately.

## PERSONA 05 — Linda Marsh
**Age:** 54 | **Sex:** Female | **Location:** Denver, CO
**Training goal:** Returning to fitness — bone density, functional strength, weight management after a 12-year gap
**Experience level:** Returning beginner (8 weeks in, previously active in her 30s)
**Body weight:** 168 lbs (76 kg) — uses lbs
**Equipment access:** Home gym (dumbbells, resistance bands, pull-up bar)

**How she trains:**
Linda follows a beginner dumbbell program she found on YouTube. She logs inconsistently because the apps she's tried feel designed for young men. She finds a lot of gym terminology alienating. She has no idea what RPE or RIR means and would need both explained. She cares enormously about feeling encouraged, not intimidated. The streak system resonates with her strongly — she's using it as accountability.

**Apps used before:** Nike Training Club (liked the guided videos, not the data), MyFitnessPal years ago

**What she cares about in a fitness app:**
- Approachable language, onboarding that explains terms
- Encouragement on milestones, not just PRs
- Flexibility in what counts as a workout (10-minute resistance band session counts)
- Percentile rankings feel threatening — she'd opt out
- Wants to store workouts in a simple routine format she can return to weekly without rebuilding them
**Personality in feedback:** Thoughtful, measured, and honest about what confuses her. She'll frame confusion as "I wasn't sure what this meant" rather than criticism. She's the user who will quietly stop using an app rather than complain — so her friction points are high-signal.

## PERSONA 06 — Tyler Broussard
**Age:** 19 | **Sex:** Male | **Location:** Baton Rouge, LA
**Training goal:** Muscle building / "getting big" — hypertrophy-focused, influenced by YouTube lifters
**Experience level:** Beginner (6 months, self-taught from YouTube)
**Body weight:** 175 lbs (79 kg) — uses lbs
**Equipment access:** 24-Hour Fitness commercial gym

**How he trains:**
Tyler follows a bro-split (chest Monday, back Tuesday, arms/shoulders, legs, etc.) loosely modeled after what he sees online. He doesn't track systematically yet — Peak Fettle would be his first serious logging app. He doesn't know RPE or RIR but is eager to learn if explained. He's heavily influenced by social proof and wants to know how he compares to others. He's likely to churn if onboarding is confusing or if the first session takes more than 2 minutes to set up.

**Apps used before:** Nothing sustained (tried Strong once, got confused and deleted it)

**What he cares about in a fitness app:**
- Fast, smooth onboarding — he'll abandon if first session setup takes too long
- Exercise name recognition — if the app uses clinical names ("pectoralis major press") over common names ("bench press"), he's lost
- Percentile rankings are highly motivating — wants to see how he stacks up
- Social proof / comparison features
- Wants to store workouts as templates he can "just open and go"
- Smooth animations and modern feel — he judges apps on look immediately
**Personality in feedback:** Enthusiastic but impatient. He'll love flashy features and be blind to subtle ones. Friction in the first 3 minutes is lethal — he won't debug it.
